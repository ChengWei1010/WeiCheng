Artism

UI/UX Design

Overview

Artism is an educational technology provided for Autism children to learn about the social interaction meaning and emotions through social stories and art therapy.

Role

UX research / UX design / UI design

Team

Wei Cheng / Yanbin Hao

Tools

Paper Prototyping / Balsamiq / Photoshop / Sketch

Methods

Interview / Observation / Wireframing

PROCESS
Design Thinking

The goal for this project is to create an educational technology for children. We didn't set a target user group because we want to open for opportunities based on observing children in the real world.

LEARN FROM THE ENVIRONMENT
Observation

We went to Austin Central Public Library to observe what kind of technology do children like to play on the computers. It turned out that preschool children (3-5) likes to interact with the art related, language-free games. The child I observed was a 4-years old girl, she was playing a game called PAINT. Through the observation, we have these takeaways:

Drawing is a good communication way.
The girl can't speak English well, but we could still feel and know what her current emotions through her painting activity.
Interacting with touchscreen.
Even though there are keyboards and mouse that specially designed for them, they still use fingers.
Feedbacks and instructions are important.
The girl couldn’t know some of the meaning of the tools. It’s better to implement appropriate instructions in technologies.

DESIGN RAIONALS
Research

We conducted literature review to find out potential related topics that we can do. According to Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), based on an analysis of 2014-2018 medical and educational records of 8-11 year-old autism’s estimated prevalence among children in US is 1 in 49 kids. Thus, we decide to set our target need group as the preschool kids (3-5 years old) with autism spectrum disorder.

Social stories are short stories that describe social situations in terms of relevant social cues and often define appropriate responses (Gray & Garand, 1993). Studies shows that for some children with ASD, social stories have been successful in improving their responses to social situations and communication skills within a short period of time.

Individuals with autism often experience anxiety, stress, depression, and frustration as a result of their symptoms and the social impact of their symptoms (Martin, 2009). Round, Baker & Rayner (2017) found that the art making process was associated with improved speech, communication, and social interaction. Therefore, due to the benefits of art therapy about emotional and behavioral development, and the goal of our technology to enhance the emotion expression, we decide to apply this method in our design.
