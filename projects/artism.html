Artism

UI/UX Design

Overview

Artism is an educational technology provided for Autism children to learn about the social interaction meaning and emotions through social stories and art therapy. I worked as a team of two people, including design process included observation, interview, research, ideate, prototype, and design.

Process

We applied the design thinking methodology as our process, but we changed a bit on the discover phase into observe and research because this project is based on the goal to create an educational technology for children and we want to open for other opportunities based on observing children in the real world.

Observation

The goal of this stage is to discover potential usage behavior and need of children in specific age range. We went to Austin Central Public Library to observe what kind of technology or game do most children like to play on the computers. It turns out that preschool children (3-5) likes to interact with the art related, language-free games. The child I observed was a 4-years old girl, the game she was playing is PAINT. From the observation, we have these takeaways:

  1. Drawing can be a communication way that can let children express themselves.
    English is not the first language of the little girl we observed, but we could still feel and know what her current emotions through her painting.
  2. Children prefer interacting with touchscreen.
    Even though there are keyboards and mouse that specially designed for children, they still prefer touching and tapping on the computer screen.
  3. Preschool kids need feedback and instructions when interact with technology.
    The girl couldn’t know some of the meaning of the tools, it’s better to implement instructions by the app itself or guidance from adults.

Interview

The goal of this interview phase is to found out their child’s use of technology and their beliefs about how technology influences children. We interviewed the little girl’s mother for 20 minutes with 5 questions and open for her own sharing. The mother told us that technology is a tool that can help kids learning in this new generation, just like books and toys. Some insights from the interview are:

  1. Parents prefer technology has educational meaning to preschool children.
    Technologies provide kids new approaches to complete task. Kids could pre-prepare for school work with skills learning from touchscreen.
  2. Painting apps help kids to learn different hand movements and hand-eye coordination.
    The physical drawing includes more senses, such as the smell of the crayon, the feel of paper, or the sounds of scratching on paper. As for painting on the computer, it included other cognitive understanding like knowing the difference between each function, and the meaning of symbols.
